Overview

This survey was conducted across the TTS QA team to understand how time is distributed across daily tasks, establish a baseline for fair target-setting, and surface operational friction. 27 QAs responded, representing 17 languages across Emotion-VA, Emotion-TX, and combined project roles. The data reveals a team that is highly engaged and self-directed, but operating under significant structural strain — platform instability, communication fragmentation, and a lack of centralized task visibility are the primary bottlenecks standing between current output and higher efficiency.

Where time goes

  • Application reviews are the most time-intensive task at ~2.1h avg/day and are universal across all QAs.
  • TTS audits, task reviews, and TX audits each average ~1.6–1.7h/day.
  • Documentation and guideline work averages ~1.5h but spikes sharply for QAs managing onboarding.
  • Community management (CB chat) averages ~1.2h but can reach 4h+ on high-traffic days.
  • Contributor troubleshooting is largely invisible in metrics but accounts for ~50min/day on average.

Target-setting baseline

  • App reviews: realistic range is 20–50/day depending on project phase. 20–30 during ramp-up; 30–50 at steady state.
  • Task reviews: most QAs complete 20–30/day; TX-heavy QAs report higher (30–50+).
  • Targets should be set with the understanding that platform bugs, onboarding surges, and community management spikes can consume 2–4h of any given day.
  • A tiered target model (by project type and phase) is more defensible than a single universal number.

Critical findings

The most consistent signal across open responses is that platform instability (Slingshot) is the single largest drain on productivity — cited by 12 of 27 QAs. Freezing, unsaved applications, cache-clearing interruptions, and missing features (notably in-platform feedback delivery) force QAs into manual workarounds that collectively cost hours per day across the team. The second major finding is that a substantial portion of QA work — onboarding prep, tracker maintenance, CB categorization, email campaigns, async reading — is not captured in any existing metric, creating a visibility gap that risks misrepresenting individual performance when targets are introduced.

High email volume

Sending individual audit feedback, onboarding confirmations, and inactive-user emails manually is slow and not scalable. In-platform feedback from Slingshot was the top requested feature.

👁️

Invisible background work

5 QAs

Deck prep, tracker maintenance, CB categorization, and async reading are not reflected in output metrics. QAs feel this work goes unrecognized and risks being overlooked when targets are set.

Recommendations

Based on survey analysis

Set tiered targets, not universal ones

App review targets should differ by project phase (ramp-up vs steady state) and role (VA vs TX). A range of 20–50/day is defensible; a single fixed number is not.

Prioritize Slingshot stability fixes

Platform bugs are the clearest systemic multiplier on lost productivity. Every freeze across 27 QAs compounds fast. Audit feedback delivery in-platform should be a near-term feature request.

Create async onboarding resources

Loom videos or recorded walkthroughs for approved contributors would reduce repetitive onboarding sessions, freeing QA time for audits and deeper contributor work.

Acknowledge non-metric work in targets

Background work (deck prep, tracker maintenance, email campaigns) should be explicitly named as part of the role when targets are communicated — so QAs don't feel penalized for doing it.

Build a shared task ownership view

A lightweight shared board (even a Google Sheet) showing who is working on what, their current load, and recent completions would reduce coordination overhead and improve visibility for leads.

Establish notification hygiene norms

Define which channels are for urgent announcements vs async discussion. Tag posts by audience (QA-VA, QA-TX). This alone could recover meaningful focus time across the team.
